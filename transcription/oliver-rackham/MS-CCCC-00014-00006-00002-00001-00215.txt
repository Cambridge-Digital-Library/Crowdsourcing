HAYLEY WOOD.
Stukeley Mere'Island was made today by joining together about 1/2 ton of maple & aspen logs from Hayley Wood with wire & staples. Held down by buried cross wires and by willow stakes at intervals. Bush of 5x 3 cinerca fetched from S. side of lake, where it had stranded, and replaced.
On the S. side of the lake are several willow bushes which have grown up probably on the smaller islands, become detached in storms, floated across, and taken root. 
Three Little Ringed Ploved seen. They make a noise like a pedestrian-crossing. 

[16758-16759]
6758
Deepest part of the main lake seems to be about 5ft - water level now being about 1/2 ft below usual.
Bottom very uneven, rising to within a few inches in places. Gravel with coarse sand and much clay. Chtophytes continuous in places' some narrow-leaved Potamogeton but otherwise little higher-plant vegetation. 
Salix alba cut for stakes: c. 3" across at 5y. growth (prob. from stool shoots a few years after original establishment). About 20ft high.
Navigated in rubber dinghy - very unmanageable in slight wind.
